Emily Longnecker Biography and Wiki

Emily Longnecker is an American WTHR award-winning journalist who was born and brought up in Northwestern, PA, United States of America. Currently, she works at WTHR (NBC affiliate) in Indianapolis, IN as a general assignment reporter. Previously, before joining WTHR, Longnecker worked as a general assignment reporter at WLWT Channel 5 for three years.

Some of her news coverage includes the “Good Samaritan Saves Baby,” the rescue story of a baby trapped in a car that went into a pond. Additionally, Emily has earned several awards including; Associated Press “Best Spot News” Award and an Emmy Award. Emily was featured in a book known as “Women Journalists at Ground Zero,” for her coverage of Flight 93’s crash in Shanksville, Pennsylvania.

Emily Longnecker WTHR

The journalist began her career at WLWT-TV which is an NBC affiliate in Cincinnati, OH where she worked for three years as a reporter. Moreover, Emily would report and anchor for stations in Lancaster and Altoona, PA, and Elmira, NY.

Before Longnecker got an on-air job, she used to work as a freelance field producer for The Washington Bureau in Washington, D.C. In Oct 2007, Longnecker moved to WTHR-TV which is an NBC-affiliated television station based in Indianapolis, IN where she works as a news reporter/anchor with the Eyewitness News team.

During her free time, she enjoys movies, discovering “mom and pop” restaurants,  spending time in the park with her Yorkshire Terrier, Luca-Brazi which is a trained therapy dog who visits nursing homes and performs tricks for residents.
