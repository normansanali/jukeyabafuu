Nancy Wilson

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Saturday, August 31, 2024

Nancy Sue Wilson, better known as Nancy Wilson, was an American vocalist who worked in the music industry for nearly five decades before retiring in the early 2010s. She was a civil rights activist as well. She died on December 13th, 2018, at the age of 81. She rose to prominence as a jazz singer. She is dubbed “the consummate entertainer” and “consummate actor.” Her music is largely influenced by jazz, R&B, pop, blues, and soul. She has released almost 70 albums and has been nominated for and won three Grammy Awards. Her hit “(You Don’t Know) How Glad I Am” and her cover of the standard “Guess Who I Saw Today” among her most well-known works.

“Sweet Nancy,” “The Baby,” “The Girl With the Honey-Coated Voice,” and “Fancy Miss Nancy” were some of her nicknames.

Net Worth of Nancy Wilson:

Nancy Wilson, a well-known jazz vocalist, has a net worth of $15 million. Her musical profession provided her with the most of her wealth.

Best Known For:

  • Her single “(You Don’t Know) How Glad I Am” and her version of the standard “Guess Who I Saw Today”.

Gossips and Rumors:

Nancy Wilson, a three-time Grammy Award winner and jazz singer, died on December 13, 2018, at the age of 81. She died in her Pioneertown, California, home. The cause of her death has yet to be determined. She, on the other hand, had been ill for a long time.

In 2006, she was admitted to the hospital with anemia and potassium shortage. She was admitted to the hospital again in 2008 due to lung issues.

Over the course of her five-decade musical career, she has published over 70 albums.

Early Life of Nancy Wilson:

Nancy Sue Wilson was born on February 20, 1937, in New York City. Lillian Ryan and Olden Wilson gave birth to her. She was born in the United States, in the city of Chillicothe. She was an American citizen. She is one of five siblings. During summer visits to her grandmother’s place, she used to sing in church choirs and at her grandmother’s house. She aspired to be a vocalist since she was four years old.

She was a student at West High School. In Ohio, she enrolled at Central State College. She did, however, drop out of college to pursue a singing career.

Career of Nancy Wilson:

In 1956, she joined Rusty Bryant’s Carolyn Club Big Band for the first time. She was chosen after an audition.

Julian “Cannonball” Adderley recommended she relocate to New York City, where she earned her first big break singing in the club “Blue Morocco.” She used to work as a secretary for the New York Institute of Technology during the day and sang at night.

Her first smash record, “Guess Who I Saw Today,” was her debut single. Like in Love was her debut album.

Her popularity grew once her R&B song “Save Your Love For Me” became a hit.

One of her most popular singles, “(You Don’t Know) How Glad I Am,” reached number 11 on the Billboard Hot 100.

On NBC, she has her own show, The Nancy Wilson Show. Her Emmy was earned for the show.

She’s been in a number of TV shows, including I Spy, The F.B.I., Room 222, Police Story, and others.

She’s also appeared on The Tonight Show, The Danny Kaye Show, The Smothers Brothers Comedy Hour, The Cosby Show, The Carol Burnett Show, The Andy Williams Show, The Flip Wilson Show, and a number of other shows as a guest.

She recorded five albums for Japanese companies in the 1980s. She also won the Tokyo Song Festivals on a regular basis.

On September 10, 2011, she had her final public performance at Ohio University in Athens, Ohio.

Awards and Honors of Nancy Wilson:

In the category of best rhythm and blues, she earned her first Grammy Award for her album How Glad I Am. R.S.V.P. (Rare Songs, Very Personal) in 2005 and Turned to Blue in 2007 won her two Grammys for Best Jazz Vocal Album.

In 1998, she was named the Playboy Reader Poll Award winner for best jazz vocalist.

She has also received the Urban League’s Whitney Young Jr. Award in 1992, the Playboy Reader Poll Award for best jazz vocalist in 1998, the World Conference of Mayors’ 1986 Global Entertainer of the Year, the Martin Luther King Jr. Center for Nonviolent Social Change’s 1993 Martin Luther King Jr. Center for Nonviolent Social Change’s 1998 NAACP Image Award-Hall of Fame Award, the 1994 Trumpet Award for Outstanding Achievement, and a number of other prestigious awards.

In 1990, she was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame at 6541 Hollywood Boulevard.

Personal Life of Nancy Wilson:

Nancy Wilson married drummer Kenny Dennis for the first time in 1960. Kenneth (Kacy) Dennis Jr., the couple’s first child, was born in 1963. In 1970, they divorced.

On May 22, 1973, she married Presbyterian preacher Reverend Wiley Burton. Samantha Burton was born in 1975, and she is their only child. In 1976, they adopted Sheryl Burton.

She died on December 13, 2018, at the age of 81, at her home in Pioneertown, California, following a protracted illness. Burton, her husband, died in 2008 from renal cancer.
